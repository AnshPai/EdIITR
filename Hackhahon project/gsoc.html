GSoC

Prerequisite

As GSoC is purely open source internship so you need to have knowledge of Git tools and how to use Github platform(because almost all projects are on Github), actually not specifically for GSoC but this a skill which I think is must for every developer and it’s better to learn it early.

Step 1: Choosing the right project and organization

To get this step right you have to go to the GSoC archives for organizations that are selected last year, look through their projects and and try to find the project that interests you, most of the organization that are selected last year are ought to be repeated. The organization that are selected for GSoC are announced almost one month prior to date when students application opens, so you got a lot of time to choose the org with the project that is best for you. It is not necessary that you totally understand every aspect of the projects, most projects are combination of different tech stack but you need to focus only your own tech. If you are a complete beginner and know a little about programming language then also there is no need to worry. Just look through the projects, shortlist the ones you find interesting, try to use their released products, think of improvement or new features, start learning the language it is using and try to implement those features. It is possible you don’t find your interest in first attempt but this process of choosing project is hit and trial, but you need to constantly keep making efforts till you find one.

Step 2: Joining the project mailing list and get yourself recognized

This step is just a sub part of first step as for every project you find on GSoC there is a channel where all the discussion related to projects are done on platforms like gitter, slack etc. You need to join that channel, and get yourself recognized by drafting a short introductory message telling about yourself and asking them how to get started. Someone from the community will surely reply to you and will guide to how to get started with the project. This is important because this is the place where you can ask all your doubts and show you presence in the community.

Step 3: Get yourself familiarized with the project codebase and start contributing

After you have chosen you project, go through their codebase on GitHub, look through their issues, find the simple issues the ones that you could solve or raise the issue on your own if you find one and start working on them, now here is one point that you need to keep in mind that Always ask before working on any issue either on channel or GitHub itself because there may be chance that somebody else is already working, it’s better to look through some old issues because there is very less probability that somebody is working on them. After choosing issues try to resolve them and if you have any doubt ask on channel and start making PR for them. Doing this will increase your for being selected.

Step 4: Start solving other people doubts in the community

When you get familiar with the codebase and start resolving issues then always try to help other with doubts, this will be your added advantage. Never try to misguide anybody because after all its a community where people code and learn together.

Step 5 Making a proposal

This is the most crucial and deciding step, when the student application period start then you get almost one month to submit your proposal. Read the guidelines on GSoC website on how to make a good proposal. Now in this proposal I will recommend explaining your work along with wireframes which you can make using online tools like (I used JustinMind Prototyper and Moqups) because wireframes make your proposal look more attractive. Keep your proposal to the point and and deliverables should satisfy the requirement mentioned under project, don't include something unnecessary and unrealistic that is gonna reduce your chance of being selected. Important: If you get in touch with one of the mentors or if possible try to meet him in person then he could help you by reviewing the draft of your proposal and providing you early feedback, this could maximize your chances to get selected.

After submitting proposal

Don’t stop contributing, because the review by mentors take upto one month more and in this time you can actually start working on some of the deliverables you mentioned on your proposal and believe me mentors take your contributions in consideration during selection procedure.
